College Selection

Why this step matters

You know your career (Step 1) and your majors (Step 2). Now build a list of colleges that fit your specific requirements — not a list copied from a ranking. A good list balances three things: your major, your family's finances, and your life circumstances (like whether you need to live at home and commute).

Understand the main categories before you start:

Public universities — the UC system, Cal State system, and UT system — offer strong programs at lower in-state cost. Cal States are often the most affordable and commuter-friendly. Private colleges — like Stanford, Santa Clara, USF, Cornell, MIT — have high sticker prices but often surprisingly generous aid: Stanford, for example, offers substantial aid for families earning under roughly $150K, and often full tuition below $100K. Never cross a private college off your list because of sticker price — check the real net price first (Step 4 covers this in depth).

Commuter options matter for many families. If living at home makes financial or family sense, confirm each school actually allows freshmen to commute — some require first-year students to live on campus. Call or email admissions and get the answer in writing. Environment and fit matter too: campus size, distance from home, class sizes, and support programs for first-generation students (like Cal State's EOP).

Your action items

  1. Write your personal criteria first, then search. Example criteria: "allows commuters starting freshman year, offers majors related to physician's assistant, and offers generous financial aid." Create a free account on CollegeVine and build your list there, cross-checking with Niche and College Board's search tools. Focus on whichever site gives you the most relevant information — you won't get everything from a single website. Aim for 8–12 schools: a mix of safety, match, and reach, with at least 2–3 affordable safeties you'd be happy to attend.
  2. Record every school in your Google Sheet with columns for: campus, your major offered (Y/N), public/private, distance from home, commuter-friendly for freshmen (Y/N — verified how?), application platform (UC / Cal State / Common App / ApplyTexas), application deadline, and estimated net price (you'll fill this in during Step 4). Your list will change over time — that's normal and expected.
  3. Review the full list with your parents and make sure they are aligned. Talk honestly about distance, commuting, and cost. If a school like Stanford is on the list, discuss first-year campus housing requirements and travel before you invest time in its application. Bring the agreed list to your next coaching meeting.
Family check-in: Any college stays on the list only if your parents are comfortable with the distance and the likely cost. Getting alignment now prevents painful surprises in April.

Trusted resources

CollegeVine
Your primary list-building tool. The free chancing engine estimates your admission odds at each school based on your GPA, courses, and activities — which helps you balance safeties, matches, and reaches honestly.
Niche
The best source for campus environment and fit: real student reviews on dorms, food, safety, professors, and campus culture. Use it to answer "would I actually be happy here?"
College Board BigFuture — College Search
Reliable official data on majors offered, size, cost, and admission requirements. A good cross-check when CollegeVine and Niche disagree.
UC Admissions — Campuses & Majors
Official UC source for comparing all nine undergraduate campuses. Pair it with Cal State's campus list — with 23 campuses, there's almost always a commuter-friendly Cal State near home.
College Navigator (U.S. Dept. of Education)
Government data with no marketing spin: graduation rates, average net price by income bracket, and student loan default rates. Check every finalist school here before it makes your final list.
Tie it to a date: Have your working list done by mid-August of senior year, before applications open in earnest. QuestBridge's National College Match deadline lands in late September — if you may qualify (Step 4), your list needs to be ready even earlier. See the Deadline Calendar.
