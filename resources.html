Resources & Templates

Everything reusable in one place: templates to copy, tools to bookmark, and a short list of things worth listening to. Each item has a note on when to use it. The step pages explain the process — this page holds the tools.

★ Start with these five essentials:
  • ★ The Master Tracker template (below) — your single command center
  • ★ BLS Occupational Outlook Handbook — every career question starts here
  • ★ studentaid.gov — FAFSA and all federal aid
  • ★ Hemingway App — simplify every essay before submitting
  • ★ The Deadline Calendar — check it monthly, verify dates yearly

1. Templates

★ Master Tracker spreadsheet (download from your coach, or download the template here)
One spreadsheet, eight tabs: Careers, Colleges, Financial Aid, Essays, Activities, Scholarships, Offers, and Deadlines — matching Steps 1–10 exactly. Import it to Google Drive (File → Open → Upload in Google Sheets), rename it with your name, and share it with your coach. Every step page tells you which tab to update.
FAFSA document checklist (inside the tracker's "Financial Aid" tab)
The exact items to gather with your parents before October 1: AGI from the latest tax return, your W-2s if you worked, and the asset table (cash/CDs, stocks/investments, 529 plans, property other than your home) for both student and parent. Details in Step 4.
Deadline tracker (the tracker's "Deadlines" tab + the Calendar page)
Copy the typical dates from the Calendar page, then verify each one on the official site for your cycle and mark it confirmed. Dates change every year — the template reminds you.

2. Essay toolkit

★ Hemingway App then Grammarly — in that order
Hemingway first, to simplify hard-to-read sentences. Grammarly second, for spelling and punctuation. Never AI to write — your essays must be your own voice, always (Step 5 explains why).
UC Personal Insight Questions guide · Common App prompts
The official prompt pages. Read them before drafting anything, and copy every prompt you'll answer into your Essays tab with its word limit and deadline.
The three rules (from your coach)
1) Write in your own voice — if it doesn't sound like you talking, revise. 2) Answer the actual question, especially in UC PIQs. 3) Read every final draft aloud to a parent or sibling before submitting.

3. Research tools

★ BLS Occupational Outlook Handbook
Use when asking: "What does this career pay, what's the job outlook, and what degree does it need?" (Step 1)
CollegeVine
Use when asking: "What are my realistic chances at this college, and how do I build a balanced list?" (Step 3)
Niche
Use when asking: "What is life actually like on this campus?" Real student reviews on environment and fit. Remember — no single website has everything; cross-check.
College Navigator
Use when asking: "What are this college's real graduation rates and net prices, without marketing?" Government data — check every finalist here.

4. Financial aid quick links

★ FAFSA · CSS Profile · CA Dream Act
The three aid applications. FAFSA for everyone; CSS Profile for many private colleges; Dream Act instead of FAFSA for eligible undocumented California students. All open October 1 — submit early (Step 4).
Net Price Calculator Center
Find every college's real cost for your family. Save each result as a PDF and log it in your Colleges tab.
Fee waivers: UC · Cal State · Common App
Based on family income you may pay $0 to apply. Check before paying any application fee.
QuestBridge
Full four-year scholarships at 50+ top colleges for high-achieving, low-income students. Check eligibility the summer before senior year — the Match deadline lands in late September.

6. Quick glossary

Net price
What your family actually pays: cost of attendance minus grants and scholarships (free money only).
AGI
Adjusted Gross Income — the income number from your parents' tax return that aid forms ask for.
SAI
Student Aid Index — the number FAFSA calculates to determine your aid eligibility.
EA vs. ED
Early Action = apply early, not binding. Early Decision = apply early, binding — you commit before comparing aid offers. Discuss ED with your parents and coach first.
REA
Restrictive Early Action (Stanford's version) — early and non-binding, but limits where else you can apply early.
EOP / EOA
Educational Opportunity Program (Cal State) — extra advising, community, and grants for first-gen and low-income students. Apply inside your Cal State application.
PIQ
Personal Insight Questions — the UC application's 4 required answers (350 words each) chosen from 8 questions.
Cal Grant
California state grant money via FAFSA or Dream Act — the March 2 priority deadline is critical.
Fee waiver
Income-based waiver of application fees — UC, Cal State, Common App, and CSS Profile all offer them.
Summer melt
Losing your spot after May 1 by missing small summer tasks (transcript, orientation, deposits). Your Enrollment tab prevents it.

7. When you're stuck

Bring it to your next coaching meeting
Confused by a link, a form, or an aid letter? Don't lose a week to worry. Note it in your Sheet, email your coach, and put it on the agenda for your next meeting.
Your school counselor
The source for local scholarships, transcript sending, fee waiver confirmation, and recommendation logistics. Visit in September, November, and January at minimum.
Dates change annually. Every deadline mentioned anywhere on this site is a typical date — verify each one on the official source for your cycle and record the confirmed date in your Deadlines tab.
