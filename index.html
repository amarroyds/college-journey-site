Welcome to Your College Journey

This guide walks you through the entire college application journey, one step at a time — the same process we follow together in coaching. It's built for students who will be the first in their family to navigate this, and for families where financial aid isn't optional but essential.

The order matters, and it may surprise you. We start with your career, not with college names. Your career points to your majors, your majors point to your colleges, and we plan the money early — before a single essay is written. Along the way, three habits carry you through:

1. Document everything in one Google Sheet. Every step adds a tab. By May, that Sheet is your complete command center — research, deadlines, essays, offers, and your final checklist.
2. Keep your parents aligned at every stage. Career, list, money, decision — each step ends with a family conversation, so there are no surprises in April.
3. Tie every task to a date. Deadlines drive this process. Each page connects its work to the Deadline Calendar — and remember, dates change annually, so verify each one for your cycle.

The 10 steps

Step 1Career ExplorationResearch careers with real data. Everything works backward from here. Step 2Major SelectionFind the majors that lead to your career — plus a backup for each. Step 3College SelectionBuild a list by YOUR criteria: major, cost, commuting, environment, fit. Step 4Financial Aid PlanningFAFSA, CSS Profile, Net Price Calculators, Dream Act, fee waivers, QuestBridge — before essays. Step 5Essay WritingCommon App, UC PIQs, EOP statements — your own voice, always. Step 6Activities DocumentationJobs, family duties, clubs — document it all once, use it everywhere. Step 7Application CompletionUC, Cal State, Common App, ApplyTexas — submit with momentum, then verify. Step 8Scholarship Search & EssaysA steady pipeline, 1–2 per week, local first. Step 9Offer Evaluation & NegotiationRead aid letters correctly, compare true costs, appeal respectfully. Step 10Enrollment DecisionDecide with your family and commit by May 1 — then finish the summer checklist.
