Step 10 of 10

Enrollment Decision — by May 1

Why this step matters

Everything you've built since Step 1 — the career research, the list, the aid planning, the essays, the appeals — leads to one decision, due by May 1 (National College Decision Day). Nearly all colleges require you to accept your offer and pay (or waive) an enrollment deposit by this date. Miss it, and a college can give your seat away.

The right choice at this point is usually clear from your Offers tab (Step 9): the school that fits your major, your family's finances, and your life circumstances. Trust the process you've followed. A school you can afford, commute to if needed, and graduate from in four years beats a "bigger name" that buries your family in debt.

And remember — deciding is not the finish line. A handful of small tasks after May 1 (deposits, housing or commuter registration, orientation, final transcript) make your enrollment real. Students lose offers every year over these small steps; that's called "summer melt," and it will not happen to you, because it's all going in your Sheet.

Your action items

  1. Make the final decision with your parents — the week of April 20 at the latest. Sit down together with the Offers tab, confirm the winner, and confirm everyone is aligned on cost, commuting/housing, and the plan for the next four years. If two schools are truly tied, visit or attend admitted-student day before deciding. Then sleep on it once — and commit.
  2. Accept exactly one offer and decline the rest — before May 1. In your chosen school's portal: accept the admission offer, submit the enrollment deposit (ask for a deposit waiver if the fee is a hardship — colleges grant these routinely for students with fee waivers), and save the confirmation as a PDF. Then politely decline your other offers so waitlisted students get their chance. Do not double-deposit at two schools — it can get both offers revoked.
  3. Complete the post-decision checklist in your Google Sheet. Add a final "Enrollment" tab with dates for: housing application or commuter registration, orientation sign-up, placement tests if required, final high school transcript sent (usually June), accepting your financial aid package in the portal (aid is offered, but you must formally accept it), any remaining verification documents for financial aid, and first tuition payment date. Check them off one by one through the summer.
Family check-in: Celebrate together — this is your family's achievement too. Then review the first-year payment schedule with your parents so the fall bill is never a surprise.
Google Sheet reminder: Your Sheet now becomes your summer checklist. It also becomes a gift: share a cleaned-up copy with a younger sibling or friend starting their own journey.

Trusted resources

Your chosen college's admitted student portal
From now on, this is your single source of truth: deposit, housing, orientation, aid acceptance, and every summer deadline live here. Check it weekly through the summer, plus the student email account the college gives you.
studentaid.gov — Accepting your aid
Explains the final aid steps most families don't know about: formally accepting each aid component, loan entrance counseling, and signing the Master Promissory Note if you take any federal loans.
WebGrants for Students (California)
If you have a Cal Grant, confirm it's assigned to your chosen school here. If you switch schools, you must update it yourself — the grant doesn't follow you automatically.
College Board BigFuture — Plan for College
Practical checklists for the summer before freshman year — orientation, placement tests, and first-semester readiness, all in one place.
