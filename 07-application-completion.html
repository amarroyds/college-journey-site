Step 7 of 10

Application Completion

Why this step matters

This is where everything comes together: your major choices (Step 2), your college list (Step 3), your fee waivers (Step 4), your essays (Step 5), and your activities list (Step 6) all get entered into the actual application systems and submitted.

You will likely juggle three or four platforms: the UC application (all UC campuses, one application), Cal State Apply (all 23 Cal States, one application), the Common App (most private colleges — Stanford, Santa Clara, USF, Cornell; note MIT uses its own portal), and ApplyTexas if UT campuses are on your list. Each has its own account, quirks, and deadlines. The strategy that works: build momentum by submitting the simplest application first — usually a Cal State — then work up to the most complex.

Your action items

  1. Create every account early — August/September, not November. UC application, Cal State Apply, Common App, and ApplyTexas if needed (MIT's portal too, if applying). Use one email address for everything, save all logins somewhere safe, and record each account in your Google Sheet. In each system, check the fee waiver section: based on family income, you may qualify to apply free (UC waives up to four campuses; Cal State and Common App have their own waivers).
  2. Submit in order of momentum, ahead of each deadline. A proven sequence: (1) Cal State application first — it's the shortest and gets you a real submission under your belt; (2) any Early Action applications by November 1 (Stanford Restrictive Early Action, MIT EA — discuss the early vs. regular decision with your coach and parents first); (3) the UC application during November, well before the deadline (target mid-November, never the final day — the system slows badly); (4) remaining Common App and ApplyTexas schools by their deadlines. Track submission status and confirmation numbers in your Sheet.
  3. Verify every application after submission. Within a week of each submission: check the college's applicant portal was created, confirm transcripts and (for privates) recommendation letters arrived, confirm test scores were sent if required, and confirm your FAFSA/CSS made it to each school. Mark each item "Verified" in your Sheet. An application isn't done when you click submit — it's done when the college confirms it's complete.
Family check-in: Before you submit any Early Decision application, sit down with your parents — ED is binding, and you commit before comparing aid offers. Early Action (non-binding) is usually the safer choice for families who need to compare financial aid. Make this decision together, with your coach, in October.
Google Sheet reminder: Your "Applications" tab is mission control now: platform, campus, major (and backup major), deadline, fee waiver used, date submitted, confirmation number, portal login, verified-complete date.

Trusted resources

UC Application
One application covers all nine UC campuses. Opens August 1; the filing window is October 1 – early December. The fee waiver (up to four campuses free) is built into the application.
Cal State Apply
One application for all 23 Cal State campuses. Opens October 1 with priority deadline around December 1. Apply for EOP inside the same application if eligible.
Common App
The platform for most private colleges. Opens August 1. Enter your activities and main essay once; add colleges and their supplements as you go. Watch each college's own deadline — they differ.
ApplyTexas
The application system for UT Austin and all Texas public universities. UT Austin's priority deadline (typically December 1, with earlier priority dates) differs from California systems — check it separately.
MIT Application Portal
MIT doesn't use the Common App — it has its own (friendlier than you'd expect) application. If MIT is on your list, add its separate account and essay questions to your Sheet early.
Tie it to a date: Accounts by September. Early Action — November 1. UC — submit by mid/late November (deadline early December). Cal State priority — around December 1. Regular Decision privates — January 1–5. Full details on the Deadline Calendar.
