Step 5 of 10

Essay Writing

Why this step matters

Your grades and courses show what you've done. Your essays show who you are — and for first-generation students especially, they're your chance to give context that numbers never capture: your responsibilities at home, your work hours, what you've built with limited resources. With the financial plan settled in Step 4, you can now give essays the focus they deserve.

Know what each application asks for:

Common App (private colleges like Stanford, Santa Clara, USF, Cornell, MIT platforms vary — most use Common App): one main personal essay (650 words) from seven prompt choices. UC application: you answer 4 of 8 Personal Insight Questions (PIQs), 350 words each — these are direct questions, not stories, so answer them directly. Cal State: no general essay, but the EOP/EOA program (Educational Opportunity Program — extra support and aid for first-generation, low-income students) requires its own short statements. Supplementary questions: most private colleges add their own short essays — list every one before you start writing so nothing surprises you in November.

The authenticity rule: Every essay must be your own words and your own voice. Never use AI to write your essays — admissions readers can tell, some colleges check, and more importantly, a generic essay wastes your one chance to be heard. Tools are for polishing, not writing: use Hemingway App to simplify sentences, then Grammarly to check spelling and punctuation. That order, every time.

Your action items

  1. Build your essay inventory in your Google Sheet. Create an "Essays" tab listing every essay and short answer for every college on your list: prompt, word limit, deadline, status (not started / drafting / in review / final). Include all supplementary questions for your private colleges (for UC, note which 4 PIQs you'll answer). Do this before writing anything — it tells you the true size of the job.
  2. Draft in Google Docs and share for review. Write your Common App essay first (it's the longest and other essays often borrow from it), then UC PIQs, then Cal State EOP/EOA statements, then supplements. Put each draft in a Google Doc, include the question at the top of the doc, and share it with your coach and a trusted teacher. Expect 3–4 rounds of revision on the main essay — start in August or September, not November.
  3. Polish with Hemingway, then Grammarly — and read it aloud. Paste your near-final draft into Hemingway App and simplify any sentence marked hard to read. Then run Grammarly for spelling and punctuation. Finally, read the essay aloud to a parent or sibling — if it doesn't sound like you talking, revise until it does.
Family check-in: Your story includes your family's story. Talking with your parents about your history — why they made the choices they made, what they hope for you — often surfaces the most powerful essay material. Share your topics with them before you write.

Trusted resources

Hemingway App
Highlights long, tangled sentences so you can simplify them. Clear, simple sentences in your own voice beat impressive vocabulary every time. Use it on every essay before submitting.
Grammarly
Catches spelling and punctuation errors after Hemingway has done the simplifying. The free version is enough. It's a spell-checker, not a ghostwriter — accept corrections, not rewrites.
UC Personal Insight Questions — official guide
The official page for all 8 PIQs, with UC's own advice on what readers look for. Remember: PIQs are answers to questions, not creative essays. Pick the 4 questions where you have the strongest real examples.
Common App essay prompts
The current year's seven prompts. Prompt 7 ("share an essay on any topic") means you can write your best story first and match it to a prompt after.
Cal State EOP
Explains the Educational Opportunity Program and its application statements. If you're first-generation or low-income, apply for EOP at every Cal State on your list — it brings advising, community, and additional grant money.
CollegeVine essay guides
Free breakdowns of each college's supplementary questions (including Stanford's) with examples of strong and weak approaches. Use for understanding the prompts — the writing stays 100% yours.
Tie it to a date: Draft the Common App essay by late September. Cal State EOP statements and UC PIQs through October, with everything essay-related wrapped by roughly November 10 — before the submission crunch. Early Action essays (Stanford REA, MIT EA) must be final by November 1. See the Deadline Calendar.
