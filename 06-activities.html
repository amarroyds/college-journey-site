Step 6 of 10

Extracurricular & Activities Documentation

Why this step matters

Every application — Common App, UC, and Cal State EOP — asks for your activities, and you'll retype the same information in different formats several times. Documenting everything once, in your Google Sheet, means Step 7 (filling in the actual applications) becomes copy-and-paste instead of a memory test at midnight before a deadline.

One thing first-generation students consistently undersell: work and family responsibilities count. A summer job, a part-time job during the school year, caring for younger siblings, translating for your parents — admissions readers value these as much as (often more than) a school club. If you worked 15 hours a week, that is your extracurricular, and it shows exactly the resilience colleges like QuestBridge partners look for.

Your action items

  1. Build your activities inventory in your Google Sheet. Create an "Activities" tab and list every summer job, internship, volunteering role, club, sport, family responsibility, and personal project from 9th grade on. Columns: activity, role/title, organization, grades participated (9/10/11/12), hours per week, weeks per year, and what you did or achieved (be specific: "trained 3 new employees," "raised $400 for the club"). This will be required for the Common App and UC applications — do it in early fall, before the application rush.
  2. Write a strong one-line description for your top activities. Common App gives you 150 characters per activity; UC gives you 350. Lead with action verbs and numbers: "Cashier & shift trainer, 12 hrs/week — trained 3 new hires, managed closing register" beats "worked at store." Draft these in the Sheet, run them through Hemingway if they feel wordy, and review them with your coach.
  3. Line up your recommendation letters (private colleges) with your activities story. UCs and Cal States don't take letters, but private colleges do. Ask two teachers who know your work well — and give each one your activities Sheet so they can write with specifics. Ask by early October at the latest; teachers get swamped by November 1 deadlines.
Google Sheet reminder: This tab does double duty — you'll reuse it for scholarship applications in Step 8, and it's the document you hand recommenders. Keep it current every semester, not just senior year.
Family check-in: Ask your parents to help you remember everything — jobs, family duties, community involvement. They often recall contributions you take for granted. Also confirm any W-2s from jobs: your work income matters for FAFSA (see Step 4).

Trusted resources

UC — Activities & Awards section guide
Official guidance on UC's six activity categories (including "Work Experience" as its own category — proof that jobs count). Shows exactly what UC readers want in each field.
Common App — first-year application guide
Explains the 10-slot activities list and honors section. Note the order matters: put your most significant activities first.
CollegeVine — activities list guides
Free articles with before/after examples of activity descriptions, including how to present work and family responsibilities powerfully.
Hemingway App
Character limits are tight — Hemingway helps you cut filler words so every character earns its place.
